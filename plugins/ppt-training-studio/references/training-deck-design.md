## Training Framework

- Selected frameworks: cognitive load theory and Mayer-style multimedia learning for slide design, Gagne's Nine Events for lesson flow, Universal Design for Learning for inclusive access, and WCAG for readable accessible output.
- Start with learning outcomes, not topics.
- Use cognitive-load discipline: remove decorative clutter, chunk content, signal what matters, and keep related words/visuals close.
- Use Mayer-style multimedia learning principles: coherence, signaling, redundancy control, spatial/temporal contiguity, segmenting, pretraining, modality, personalization, and worked examples.
- Alternate explanation with practice, reflection, checks, and scenarios.
- Include facilitator notes and learner handouts when the deck will be taught.
- Use Gagne when building full lessons: gain attention, state objective, recall prior knowledge, present content, guide learning, practice, feedback, assess, and transfer.
- Use UDL to vary representation, engagement, and learner action/expression without lowering the learning goal.

## Slide System

- Lesson opener: outcome, relevance, time, prerequisites.
- Concept slide: one concept, visual explanation, short narration.
- Worked example: step-by-step reveal, annotated screenshot/code/process.
- Practice slide: task, timer, instructions, success criteria.
- Knowledge check: question, options, feedback, explanation.
- Scenario: context, choice, consequence.
- Summary: 3-5 takeaways and next action.

## Design System

- Theme: approachable, clear, inclusive, calm.
- Colors: high contrast, consistent semantic colors for concept/practice/check/warning.
- Fonts: readable at classroom and screen-share sizes.
- Icons: use for recurring learning modes, not decoration.
- Charts: teach the interpretation; annotate the insight.
- Smart graphics: use for process, cycles, taxonomies, decision trees, and timelines.
- Video: short, purposeful, captioned or summarized, with pause prompts and fallback stills.

## QA Gates

- Outcomes are measurable.
- Each lesson chunk has explain, show, practice, check.
- Slides do not overload working memory.
- Important visuals have alt text or narration support.
- Accessibility and inclusive language are checked.
