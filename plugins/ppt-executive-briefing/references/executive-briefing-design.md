# Executive Briefing Design Reference

Keep this reference lean. Do not bulk-load external source pages because a source URL exists; browse only topic-specific sources needed for evidence, images, market data, figures, or citations.

## Executive Deck Purpose

Executive decks create decision clarity. They should be answer-first, evidence-led, visually credible, and fast to parse. Strong executive decks do not look generic: finance, mining, software, healthcare, industrial, and education stories need different visual systems.

## Required Design Brief

Before creating slides, define:

- Audience and decision: who decides, what they need to approve or understand.
- Domain theme: palette, chart grammar, icons, image style, footer/source treatment.
- Proof stack: metrics, charts, screenshots, customer evidence, market maps, product images, diagrams, or source figures.
- Risk/ask treatment: where the explicit decision, tradeoff, and risk appear.
- Asset plan: official/user-provided/open assets, generated visuals, or recreated source concepts.

## Visual System

- Use fewer objects, stronger hierarchy, clear whitespace, and direct labels.
- Use charts only when they answer the executive question; title charts with the takeaway.
- Use icons sparingly for navigation, capabilities, categories, or risk states.
- Use real product screenshots, market maps, asset photos, customer evidence, or official visuals when they materially increase credibility.
- Avoid generic feature cards and decorative icon grids.

## Domain Theme Examples

- Finance/IR: navy/green, precise tables, source footers, bridge charts, disclosure style.
- Software/platform: product screenshots, architecture or workflow maps, crisp line icons, code/grid cues.
- Metals/mining/industrial: graphite/copper, asset/process imagery, operations maps, safety/risk markers.
- Healthcare/science: clinical palette, protocol/pathway diagrams, evidence markers, human impact visuals.
- Education/training: approachable palette, learner journey, outcomes, practice/check motifs.

## QA Gates

- Can the main message be understood in 30 seconds?
- Is the ask or decision explicit?
- Are key numbers exact, sourced, and unit-consistent?
- Does every chart have a takeaway in the title?
- Does the deck include credible domain-specific proof objects?
- Is appendix material separated from the core narrative?
- Does it look boardroom-ready at thumbnail and full-screen scale?
