# Pitch Board Deck

Use this skill for persuasive executive decks where narrative rhythm and credibility matter.

Read `../../references/executive-briefing-design.md` before substantial deck work. Read `../../references/powerpoint-production-handoff.md` before creating a PPTX.

## Narrative Workflow

1. Define audience: board, investor, customer, executive team, partner, or hiring panel.
2. Define desired action: approve, fund, buy, align, unblock, or investigate.
3. Build story arc: market/problem, insight, solution, proof, model, traction, plan, risks, ask.
4. Replace generic feature lists with proof: customer quotes, screenshots, metrics, pipeline, pilots, benchmarks, demos, financial model, or sourced market evidence.
5. Lock a domain visual system and image/icon plan before slide creation.
6. Use high-contrast chapter slides for rhythm.
7. Keep visuals native and editable when possible; use real assets when they prove the story.

## Gate

The deck must answer: why now, why this, why us, why believe, and what decision is needed.
