# Pitch Board Deck

Use this skill for persuasive executive decks where narrative rhythm and credibility matter.

Read `../../references/executive-briefing-design.md` before substantial deck work.

## Narrative Workflow

1. Define audience: board, investor, customer, executive team, partner, or hiring panel.
2. Define desired action: approve, fund, buy, align, unblock, or investigate.
3. Build story arc: market/problem, insight, solution, proof, model, traction, plan, risks, ask.
4. Replace generic feature lists with proof: customer quotes, screenshots, metrics, pipeline, pilots, benchmarks, demos, or financial model.
5. Use high-contrast chapter slides for rhythm.
6. Keep visuals native and editable when possible.

## Design Patterns

- Hero claim slide.
- Market map.
- Before/after workflow.
- Traction chart.
- Competitive matrix.
- Business model flywheel.
- Roadmap.
- Ask slide.

## Gate

The deck must answer: why now, why this, why us, why believe, and what decision is needed.
